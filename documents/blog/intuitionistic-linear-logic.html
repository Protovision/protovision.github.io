Intuitionistic Linear Logic

by Mark Swoope
2024-09-09

Much of this blog is part of my independent research in logic, type theory, and category theory; I may get facts wrong. Here I cover my understanding of intuitionistic linear logic (ILL) which is a fragment of linear logic (LL) that includes these symbols: ⊤, 1 , 0, !, ⊸, &, ⨂, ⨁. ILL does not include: ⊥, ?, ⅋.

There is a style of proofs called natural deduction which intuitionistic logic (IL) is based on. In this style, every judgement can have multiple premises but only one conclusion.

In LL, ⨂ performs conjunction for premises and ⅋ performs conjunction for conclusions. Therefore, ILL removes ⅋, it's unit ⊥, and ? (which allows contraction for conclusions).

ILL also removes direct linear negation but provides ⊸.

To casually explain these connectives:

  • A ⊸ B - Lollipop: Consume A to produce B
  • A & B - With: Choose to produce A or B
  • A ⨂ B - Times: Production of both A and B
  • A ⨁ B - Plus: Production of either A or B

⊤ is the unit for &, 1 is the unit for ⨂, and 0 is the unit for ⨁.

! is the non-linear modality, enabling it's resource to be reused or unused.

It can be said that ⨂ and ⨁ are eager because they do not require consumer interaction in order to be constructed and fully evaluated.

& and ⊸ are lazy because & delays evaluation of either component until the consumer chooses and ⊸ delays the evaluation of it's result until it's demanded argument is provided.

& and ⨂ are two ways to perform conjunction. They are both eliminated by one context in one timeline. They differ in construction:

  • & is constructed from one context in disjoint timelines.
  • ⨂ is constructed from disjoint contexts in one timeline.
